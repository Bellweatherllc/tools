What We're Doing & Why

EOS calls for core processes to be "followed by all" — but not all processes carry equal weight. This exercise identifies the critical 20%: the bullets that, if executed flawlessly every time by everyone who touches them, would drive 80% of your financial health, risk management, client satisfaction, and employee engagement.

This isn't a ranking of importance — everything in the handbook matters. This is a focus exercise. Once you achieve mastery of the 20%, you revisit the list and decide what to tackle next. The goal: 25–30 Super Yes items with broad Leadership Team consensus.

Voting Rules by Round

  • Round 1Vote Yes or No on every bullet. Super Yes = 4–5 votes (locked). Yes = 3 votes (advances to Round 2). No = 0–2 votes (eliminated). Tag each Yes with its primary driver.
  • Round 2Re-vote on 3-vote Yes items only. Items reaching 4–5 votes become Super Yes and lock in. Items dropping below 3 are eliminated. If Super Yes count lands 25–30 — done.
  • Round 3Each scorer gets exactly 10 Yes votes to allocate across remaining candidates. Forces genuine prioritization.
  • Round 4Each scorer gets exactly 6 Yes votes. Final push. After Round 4, Leadership Team makes any final adjustments in group discussion.
