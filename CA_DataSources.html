Required inputs — the CA cannot generate without these
Design Agreement Needed
Sets the client-facing scope commitments the CA must honor. Informs the cover page summary language, the framing of inclusions and exclusions, and the tone of Section 15 Additional Terms.
Without this: Cover language will be generic. Exclusions and assumptions may not match what was promised in the DA.
.docx The DA extractor requires .docx. Signed PDFs can be uploaded manually but cannot be auto-extracted — use the .docx version when available.

Plan Notes Needed
Project-specific facts that only live in the drawings: tile pattern and orientation, floating shelf locations, island configuration, backsplash boundaries, dormer details, structural specifics.
Without this: Drawing-specific bullets will be missing or flagged as placeholders. Tile patterns, shelf and island details, and structural notes will not appear.

BuilderTrend Selections Needed
Distributes approved materials into the correct trade sections — cabinetry into Section 06, tile into 08.2, plumbing fixtures into 09, and so on. Also supplies dollar amounts and allowances for Section 13 Selections.
Without this: Material names will be missing from trade sections. Section 13 will have no selections listed and no allowance amounts.

Reference CAs Needed
Past construction agreements used as quality and scope benchmarks. Comp CAs — the projects whose estimates informed this one — are the baseline. Add any additional CAs from memory where the scope, client type, or trade mix overlaps with this project, even if they weren't formal comps.
Without this: Generate will produce scope language without reference to Bellweather's established patterns. The more relevant CAs attached, the closer the generated baseline will be to what you'd write yourself.
.docx.pdf Find comp CAs in the Customers folder via Sherpa, or drag in directly. Add additional CAs from institutional memory — prior projects with similar bathrooms, kitchens, structural work, or client profile.

Critique Meeting Notes Optional
Key risks, red flags, constraints, and decisions from the team crit session. Applied against section generation — a range hood restriction resolves the HVAC flag; an appliance note triggers the right rough-in language.
Add or supplement notes from the crit session not already captured in Project Setup.
Engineering / Structural Drawings Optional
Beam specifications, structural details, and engineering notes on a separate structural set. Critical when Section 03 Masonry & Structural or structural Rough Carpentry is in scope.
If structural work is in scope: Sections 03 and 04 will rely on scope outline language only. Engineer-specific dimensions and beam specs will be missing.
